Formative AI Scaffolding

A Framework for Studying How AI Shapes Adult Identity

Position Paper · December 2025

Millions of adults now use conversational AI to navigate major life decisions—career changes, relationship questions, geographic moves, and fundamental uncertainties about who they might become. This use is widespread and largely unstudied. Existing research frameworks, developed for K-12 educational contexts, don't fit: they assume predetermined curricula, bounded timeframes, and external assessment. Adults using AI for life decisions operate without these structures.

This position paper proposes formative AI scaffolding as a framework for studying this phenomenon. The core question: does AI interaction prepare people for membership in human communities, or does it substitute for the relationships that human development requires?

The Community Criterion

Human development occurs in relational contexts. Identity emerges through participation in communities of practice, relationship, and purpose. The framework proposes that AI scaffolding is formative when it prepares people for this participation—and habit-forming when it substitutes for it.

The distinction matters because design choices influence outcomes. Interface decisions, interaction patterns, and system architectures shape what users experience as possible. A system that periodically asks users what human communities they're preparing to join embeds a different orientation than one that optimizes purely for engagement.

Research Directions

The framework opens several tractable research directions for computational media and HCI:

Direction Question
Design Feature Analysis How do specific features (conversational persistence, emotional disclosure handling, future visibility) correlate with movement toward or away from community?
Trajectory Studies Longitudinal tracking of adults using AI for life decisions—do they join communities, seek mentors, rebuild relationships?
Transition Points When do users move from AI support to human support? What facilitates or impedes these transitions?
Intervention Design Can systems be designed to promote formative outcomes? What design hypotheses are worth testing?

Connection to Portfolio

This framework emerged from observing how technology mediates human development in my other work. At F.E.A.S.T., I built computational scaffolding that sequences crisis information for caregivers—design choices about timing and sequence shape what actions feel possible. At KĀʻEO, I co-designed an AI Lab where the central principle is that humans ARE the loop, not checkpoints within an automated process. Both projects raised questions about when technology prepares people for human connection versus when it substitutes for what they actually need.
