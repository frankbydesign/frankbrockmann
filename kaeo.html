AI Tools for Hawaiian Language Assessment

KĀEO is the only Hawaiian-medium assessment system in the world—and the only native language assessment used for federal accountability in the US. I've worked with the program for nearly a decade, and in 2024, co-designed an AI workflow that solved a problem nobody else had cracked.

The Problem

Psychometricians analyzing test performance don't speak Hawaiian. Hawaiian language educators aren't trained in psychometrics. When a test item performs poorly, figuring out why requires both perspectives—but they couldn't easily work together. Geography, language barriers, and the need to protect Hawaiian content created bottlenecks that slowed everything down.

The Solution

We built an AI lab—a secure environment where language models help bridge the gap between statistical analysis and cultural expertise. The key insight: AI works here not as replacement but as translator and aggregator, always under human oversight.

How it works: NotebookLM processes psychometric reports and flags patterns across dozens of items. Claude translates technical findings into plain-language briefs. Hawaiian educators review everything before it goes anywhere. Nothing leaves without cultural sign-off.

Diagram: Three-tier workflow with human checkpoints

"Hawaiian language data has been extracted and exploited before. We designed the system so nothing leaves the room without cultural review. AI as augmentation, not automation—humans are the loop, not just in it."

Results

In October 2024, we analyzed 58 flagged items across Hawaiian Language Arts, Math, and Science. Six interpretive briefs identified real issues: linguistic ambiguity, depth-of-knowledge misalignment, structural overload.
