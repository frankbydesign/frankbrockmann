Research Agenda

How do computational systems reshape human practices of care, learning, and identity formation? My work investigates the algorithmic mediation of critical human experiences—from parents seeking crisis support to indigenous communities protecting linguistic sovereignty.

I approach these questions through practice-based research: designing real-world computational systems, then analyzing how those systems mediate human experience, agency, and cultural practices.

System Architecture

Investigating Database Schema as Care Infrastructure

How does database design limit or enable agency for families in crisis? Research through designing CRM architecture, automated communication sequences, and analytics dashboards for 15,000 families annually navigating eating disorder treatment.

Key Question: What changes when "helping someone" becomes a computational process?

Human-AI Collaboration

Designing Algorithmic Workflows for Indigenous Linguistic Sovereignty

Can AI serve indigenous language assessment while preserving cultural authority? Co-designed secure AI lab enabling cross-language psychometric analysis of Hawaiian language assessments while maintaining community control over protected content through multi-expert human oversight.

Key Question: How can computational systems resist colonial patterns of knowledge extraction?
