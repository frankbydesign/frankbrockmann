Background

During my recovery from a hit-and-run injury in 2024, I used AI tools extensively: over a thousand conversations across more than six months. Somewhere in that process, a question emerged that I couldn't shake: was this helping me reconnect with people, or was it replacing the relationships I actually needed?

I looked for research that addressed this and mostly didn't find it. The literature on AI scaffolding focuses on students and curricula. But millions of adults are using AI to figure out their lives, not just their homework.

These two papers are my attempt to frame the question:

Formative AI Scaffolding proposes a criterion for distinguishing AI use that prepares people for community from AI use that substitutes for it.

Beyond Capabilities examines the same tension from an organizational angle: how mission-driven groups might adopt AI without losing what matters.

I don't know if this is exactly the right frame, but these are starting points rather than conclusions. The question connects to what I've seen at KĀʻEO and F.E.A.S.T., where design choices shape what actions feel possible for people navigating difficult transitions.
