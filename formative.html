Formative AI Scaffolding

A Framework for Studying AI's Role in Adult Identity and Community Connection

Position Paper · December 2025

When does AI interaction prepare people for richer human connection, and when does it substitute for the relationships that human development requires?

This paper proposes the community criterion as a way to distinguish between the two. AI scaffolding is formative when it moves people toward membership in human communities. It is habit-forming when the loop closes on itself: more conversation, more refinement, but no forward movement toward actual people.

The distinction matters because millions of adults now use AI to navigate career changes, relationship questions, and fundamental uncertainties about who they might become. Existing research frameworks, developed for K-12 educational contexts, don't fit. They assume predetermined curricula, bounded timeframes, and external assessment. Adults using AI for life decisions operate without these structures.

Paper Structure

The paper proceeds in six sections:

  1. Introduction establishes the phenomenon and the gap in existing research
  2. AI Scaffolding in Educational Contexts reviews what we know and where that knowledge stops applying
  3. The Gap examines how adults actually use AI for life decisions and why educational frameworks don't fit
  4. The Framework defines formative AI scaffolding and the community criterion
  5. Research Directions outlines tractable questions for computational media and HCI researchers
  6. Conclusion addresses the stakes of getting this right

Connection to Portfolio

This framework emerged from questions raised by my other work. At F.E.A.S.T., I built systems that sequence crisis information for caregivers based on what they can absorb at each stage. Design choices about timing shape what actions feel possible. At KĀʻEO, I co-designed an AI Lab where humans ARE the loop, not checkpoints in an automated process. Both projects made visible how computational systems mediate human decision-making in high-stakes contexts.

The Formative AI Scaffolding framework turns that observation into a research program: studying how design choices in AI systems influence whether users move toward human community or away from it.
